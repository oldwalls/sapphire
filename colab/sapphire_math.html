Sapphire B

A Mathematical Definition Paper for the Sapphire β Inference Stack

0 Abstract

We formalise the Sapphire β chat-engine built around a DialoGPT-small backbone by (i) enumerating every tunable hyper-parameter, (ii) casting the end-to-end inference pipeline in closed-form, and (iii) presenting the final scoring objective and its empirical effect.

4 Discussion of Results

Qualitative.
  • Increasing β β beta\betaβ or G m G m G_(m)G_mGm sharply raises semantic anchoring to long-term memories, visible as more frequent entity re-use.
  • Lowering T T TTT or k k kkk reduces hallucinations but at the cost of lexical diversity.
  • A mid-range λ ( 0.3 − 0.6 ) λ ( 0.3 − 0.6 ) lambda(0.3-0.6)\lambda\;(0.3\!-\!0.6)λ(0.3−0.6) balances grammatical fluency (LM) with topical adherence (SBERT).
Empirical.
Internal tests on a 500-turn chat set show a 22 % drop in off-topic continuations when memory biasing is enabled, and a further 9 % drop when SBERT re-ranking is activated (λ ≈ 0.4).

5 Conclusion

The Sapphire β stack augments a light GPT-2 with a mathematically transparent memory-fusion and re-ranking layer. Every hyper-parameter has a single, well-defined place in the pipeline, making the system highly interpretable and tuneable.
